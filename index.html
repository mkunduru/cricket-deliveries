Cricket

A visual guide to deliveries

1/10

Off Spin

An off spin delivery is bowled when the fingers roll down the right side of the ball giving the ball a clockwise spin as seen from behind.

2/10

Doosra

Trick throw that starts out like a normal off break, but then unexpectedly curves to the leg side. Invented by Saqlain Mushtaq.

3/10

Leg Spin

A leg spin delivery is bowled when the wrist is rotated to the left and the ball flicked by the ring finger, giving the ball an anti-clockwise spin as seen from behind.

4/10

Topspinner

A topspinner is released over the top of the fingers in such a way that it spins forward in the air towards the batsman in flight. The ball drops shorter, falls faster and bounces higher.

5/10

Slider

A slider is released by rolling the fingers down the back of the ball The ball carries to a fuller length and bounces less than expected.

6/10

Bouncer

The ball is bounced halfway to the batsman by a fast bowler so that it reaches the batsman above chest level. This delivery is used to surprise or scare a batsman.

7/10

Beamer

This is an illegal delivery that reaches the batsman above the waist level without bouncing on the track. Two of these deliveries by the same bowler results in an ejection.

8/10

Yorker

A yorker is a ball bowled which hits the cricket pitch around the batsman's feet. The yorker is difficult to play because it arrives faster and provides no room for the batsman to swing the bat.

9/10

Inswinger

An inswinger is a delivery from a bowler that curves towards the legs of a batsman while traveling through the air.

10/10

Outswinger

An outswinger is a delivery from a bowler that curves away from the batsman while traveling through the air.
